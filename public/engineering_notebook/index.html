Summary - 4511 Maker Industries

Maker Industries is a student-led robotics team from the heart of La Salle Catholic College Preparatory's maker space. We have a passion for solving material problems in our community, and we wish to inspire the next generation of thinkers and creators by promoting a fun and safe environment for creativity in engineering. In robotics we strive for reliability above all else and welcome unexpected challenges and solutions along the way.

Engineering Process

Most of our members found Robotics through La Salle’s Maker Space and Design Center, where the program is primarily based. Because of this, most of our members have taken our Introduction to Design Thinking and Tools class which introduces the design thinking process. However, the empathizing step is not necessary given that the goals are laid out at the beginning of the competition and the nature of the competition itself. Given our size and budget constraints, the rest of the design thinking process works perfectly for developing our robots to make the best robot possible.

At the beginning of the season we decided that we should have a modular design leading to our original version of one of the robots. CAD/Fusion 360

Much of this season’s development and iteration speed could not have been without using Fusion 360, our team’s preferred CAD software. This allowed us to explore new concepts without needing to physically assemble these subassemblies and allowed for a more rapid prototyping process. However, we decided that we should not model any small details because that would not be necessary for basic construction purposes.

Prototype

Designing our robot in CAD gave us the ability to disconnect the designing and building process from the assembly process. This in turn allowed us to increase the size of our team to include less experienced members in the process and to train them on how our robot system worked. Just having some simple printed schematics also allowed us to further the time we spent on the robot to include the breaks between classes and our lunchtimes.

Outreach Activities

Electronics Drive

In working very closely with our Earth Club we were able to organize one of the collection events of our school. Through our research with Earth Club, we found Free Geek, a local computer recycle and upcycle organization, that has electronics drive services they offer. After a few months of preparation, we had our first electronic recycling collection for about a week where we received many items from VHS machines to old computer towers.

Faith Service Community Fair

This event is organized by the school in order to make it easier for students to explore different experiences at our school. This year our fair was outside on the track to allow for adequate ventilation. Because of the nice material we were able to demonstrate our robot prototype for others and invite new members to join the club.

Open House

Every year our school has an open house in which the students are asked if they would like to lead and demonstrate their favorite areas of the school. In participating we were able to show seventh and eighth graders that we have a serious robotics program that is in the middle of ramping up in scale. Many students are surprised at the scale of our current program and its current trajectory.

Future Falcon Night (For future students)

This event is also organized by our school where students who have applied to the school get to start learning how the school works. At these meetings, future students often have specific questions about their hobbies and interests and how they correlate to the school. By participating in this we were able to help bring in the next generation of roboticists to ensure our sustainability far into the future as we start working on starting a girls FTC team and potentially an FRC team.
